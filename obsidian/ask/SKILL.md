## Steps

1. **Understand the question** — what topic, concept, or decision is the user asking about?

2. **Search the vault** — search broadly for relevant notes. Use multiple keyword searches covering:
   - Direct terms from the question
   - Related concepts and synonyms
   - Names of people, projects, or tools that might be relevant

3. **Read the most relevant notes** — read the content of the top candidates, follow wikilinks if they lead to more relevant material.

4. **Synthesize an answer** — answer the question based on what's in the vault. Be direct. If the vault has a clear answer, give it. If it's partial, say what's there and what's missing.

5. **Format the response:**
   - Answer first, sources after
   - Reference notes as [[wikilinks]]
   - If the vault has conflicting or evolving thoughts on the topic, surface that tension
   - If the vault has nothing useful on the topic, say so plainly — don't make things up

6. **Suggest a follow-up** — one sentence: what would be worth capturing or exploring next based on this question.
